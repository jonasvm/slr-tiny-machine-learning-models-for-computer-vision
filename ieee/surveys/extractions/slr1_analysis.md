# 2. Scope and Purpose of the Review

## Main Objective

To provide a comprehensive overview of Deepfake detection research by analyzing existing detection techniques, datasets, and evaluation metrics.

## Problem Addressed

The rapid development of Deepfake technologies introduces risks such as misinformation, cybercrime, and privacy violations. The review aims to consolidate existing detection approaches and identify research directions.

## Scope

The review focuses on Deepfake detection techniques in:

- Computer vision
- Digital media forensics
- AI-based manipulation detection
- Deep learning detection methods

## Main Contributions

1. Comprehensive review of Deepfake detection literature.
2. Taxonomy of detection techniques including:
   - Deep learning approaches
   - Machine learning methods
   - Statistical approaches
   - Blockchain-based methods.
3. Analysis of experimental evidence and performance metrics.
4. Identification of research challenges and future directions.

# 4. Inclusion and Exclusion Criteria

## Inclusion Criteria

Studies were included if:

- Search phrases appear in title, abstract, or keywords.
- If not found there, the keywords could appear elsewhere in the paper.
- The study contains empirical evidence related to Deepfake detection.

## Exclusion Criteria

Studies were excluded if:

- Not written in English.
- Duplicate versions of the same work exist.
- The study focuses on audio or text manipulation instead of image/video.
- The study focuses only on transformation techniques rather than detection methods.

---

# 5. Deduplication and Screening Process

Duplicate studies were handled by selecting the **most comprehensive version** of a work (e.g., journal version instead of conference version).

The selection process involved:

1. Retrieval of papers from databases.
2. Initial filtering using selection criteria.
3. Full-text reading of candidate papers.
4. Removal of duplicates and irrelevant works.
5. Final selection of primary studies.

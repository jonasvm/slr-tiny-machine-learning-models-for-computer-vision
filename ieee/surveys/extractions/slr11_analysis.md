# SLR11 Analysis

## 1. Basic Information

**Title:**  
Opportunities, Applications, and Challenges of Edge-AI Enabled Video Analytics in Smart Cities: A Systematic Review

**Authors:**  
Elarbi Badidi, Karima Moumane, Firdaous El Ghazi

**Year:**  
2023

**Journal / Conference:**  
IEEE Access

**Type of Review:**  
Systematic Literature Review

---

## 2. Scope and Purpose of the Review

**Main Objective:**  
To analyze the current state of research on Edge-AI-enabled video analytics in smart cities, focusing on technological developments, application domains, and the challenges associated with deploying AI-driven video analytics at the edge.

**Problem Addressed:**  
Although video analytics is widely used in smart cities, many solutions rely heavily on cloud infrastructure. The review addresses the need to understand how Edge AI can support real-time video analytics while reducing latency, bandwidth usage, and privacy risks.

**Scope:**  
- Edge AI  
- Video analytics  
- Smart cities  
- Deep learning and machine learning for video processing  
- Edge computing platforms

**Main Contributions Claimed by the Authors:**

- A systematic overview of research on Edge-AI-based video analytics.
- Identification of major application domains for edge video analytics in smart cities.
- Analysis of architectures and technologies used for deploying AI models on edge devices.
- Discussion of opportunities and challenges in deploying Edge AI for video analytics.
- Identification of future research directions in this field.

---

## 3. Search Methodology

### Databases Used

The literature search was conducted using the following databases:

- IEEE Xplore  
- Scopus  
- Web of Science  
- Google Scholar

### Search Strings

The authors defined eight search queries used to retrieve relevant literature:

1. ("Edge AI" OR "edge intelligence") AND "Video Analytics"  
2. ("AI" OR "edge intelligence") AND "Video Analytics"  
3. ("AI" OR "artificial intelligence") AND "Video Analytics"  
4. "Edge Video Analytics"  
5. "Deep learning" AND "Video Analytics"  
6. "Machine learning" AND "Video Analytics"  
7. ("smart" OR "intelligent") AND "Video Analytics"  
8. "Privacy" AND "Video Analytics"

### Time Range

The primary time window considered was:

2018 – 2023

Earlier papers (before 2017) were used only for background context.

### Study Types Included

- Journal articles  
- Conference papers

---

## 4. Inclusion and Exclusion Criteria

### Inclusion Criteria

- Studies related to Edge AI and video analytics.
- Papers addressing video analytics in smart cities or edge computing environments.
- Journal and conference publications relevant to the research topic.
- Studies published between 2018 and 2023.

### Exclusion Criteria

- Papers not related to edge-based video analytics.
- Studies focusing only on cloud-based video analytics.
- Irrelevant studies outside the scope of the review.
- Duplicate papers retrieved from multiple databases.

---

## 5. Deduplication and Screening Process

The study selection process consisted of multiple filtering stages:

1. Initial search across the four selected databases.
2. Removal of duplicate papers.
3. Screening of titles to identify relevant studies.
4. Screening of abstracts to further assess relevance.
5. Full-text analysis of candidate papers.
6. Final selection of papers included in the review.

Duplicate removal is explicitly described.  
Title, abstract, and full-text screening stages are reported.

---

## 6. PRISMA or Selection Flow

The paper includes a PRISMA-style selection diagram describing the filtering process.

Stages of the selection process include:

Identified → After duplicate removal → Screened → Eligible → Included

Reported counts:

- Initial papers identified: 752  
- After duplicate removal: 408  
- After topic filtering: 282  
- Final papers included in the review: 112

## 7. Data Extraction Method

For each selected study, the authors extracted several attributes, including:

- Application domain

- Dataset used
- Hardware platform
- Training dataset
- Accuracy
- Precision
- Latency
- Frame rate
- Power consumption
- Memory usage

These attributes were used to build comparative tables summarizing the literature.

## 11. Practical Recommendations

The authors suggest several directions for future research and practical improvements:

- Development of more efficient AI models suitable for edge devices.
- Improvement of energy efficiency for edge AI deployments.
- Better integration between AI algorithms and edge computing hardware.
- Enhancement of privacy-preserving techniques in video analytics.
- Increased collaboration between academia and industry for deploying edge AI solutions in smart cities.

---

## 12. Limitations of the Review

The authors acknowledge several limitations:

- The review does not claim to include every existing study in the domain.
- The search was limited to selected databases.
- The analysis focuses mainly on recent research (2018–2023).

---

## 13. Methodological Strengths of This SLR

Key methodological strengths include:

- Use of multiple academic databases.
- Clearly defined search queries.
- Explicit time window for included studies.
- Structured multi-stage screening process.
- Use of a PRISMA-style selection diagram.
- Large set of included papers (112 studies).
- Structured comparison tables summarizing literature.

---

## 14. Potential Methodological Weaknesses

Potential methodological limitations include:

- Absence of a formal quality assessment of included studies.
- Use of Google Scholar, which may introduce non-peer-reviewed results.
- Search queries not reported separately for each database.
- Mostly qualitative synthesis rather than quantitative analysis.

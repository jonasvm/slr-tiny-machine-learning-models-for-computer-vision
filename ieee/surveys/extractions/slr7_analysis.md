## 2. Scope and Purpose of the Review

**Main Objective:**  
To provide a comprehensive review of the evolution of YOLO object detection architectures, covering developments from YOLOv1 through YOLOv8 and YOLO-NAS.

**Problem Addressed:**  
Existing review papers only covered earlier YOLO versions (such as YOLOv3 or YOLOv4), leaving out more recent developments. The paper aims to provide an updated and more complete overview of the YOLO family of models.

**Scope:**  
- Object detection  
- Deep learning  
- Computer vision  
- YOLO architecture evolution

**Main Contributions Claimed by the Authors:**  
- Detailed review of YOLO architecture evolution.  
- Explanation of architectural innovations across YOLO versions.  
- Comparison of multiple YOLO variants.  
- Discussion of performance trade-offs between speed and accuracy.  
- Discussion of potential future research directions.

## 7. Data Extraction Method

The paper extracts and summarizes technical characteristics of YOLO models rather than systematically extracting structured data from studies.

Examples of extracted information include:

- YOLO model version  
- year of publication  
- anchor usage  
- framework used (Darknet, PyTorch, etc.)  
- backbone architecture  
- performance metrics (Average Precision)  
- dataset used (e.g., VOC2007, COCO2017)  
- architectural improvements and training strategies

A comparison table summarizes multiple YOLO architectures with fields such as version, framework, backbone, and AP performance.

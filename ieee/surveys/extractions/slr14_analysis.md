## 2. Scope and Purpose of the Review

**Main Objective of the Review**

To review and analyze existing techniques for underwater image processing, focusing particularly on image enhancement and restoration methods using both traditional image processing techniques and artificial intelligence approaches.

**Problem the Review Claims to Solve**

Underwater images are significantly degraded due to environmental factors such as light absorption, scattering, turbidity, and color distortion. These degradations make tasks such as object detection, marine exploration, and underwater monitoring challenging. The review aims to summarize methods developed to mitigate these issues.

**Scope**

The review focuses on:

- Underwater image enhancement
- Underwater image restoration
- Artificial intelligence techniques applied to underwater imaging
- Computer vision methods used in underwater environments

Technologies covered include:

- CNN-based models
- GAN-based models
- Traditional image processing techniques

Applications discussed include:

- Marine exploration
- Underwater robotics
- Environmental monitoring
- Archaeological exploration
- Military reconnaissance

**Main Contributions Claimed by the Authors**

- A comprehensive overview of underwater image enhancement and restoration techniques
- A review of traditional and AI-based underwater image processing approaches
- Comparison of several algorithms and models used in underwater vision tasks
- Discussion of datasets and evaluation metrics used in underwater imaging research
- Identification of research challenges and future directions

## 14. Potential Methodological Weaknesses

From a systematic review perspective, the paper has several methodological limitations:

- No search strategy described
- No database list provided
- No search strings reported
- No defined time window for literature selection
- No inclusion or exclusion criteria
- No screening workflow
- No PRISMA diagram
- No description of deduplication
- No quality assessment of studies

Therefore, the paper should be considered a **narrative survey rather than a formal Systematic Literature Review (SLR).**

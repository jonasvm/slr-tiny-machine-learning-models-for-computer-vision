## 2. Scope and Purpose of the Review

**Main objective of the review:**  
To analyze the convergence of edge computing and artificial intelligence in unmanned aerial vehicle (UAV) systems and explore how edge AI can enable more efficient, autonomous, and scalable UAV applications.

**Problem the review claims to solve:**  
Traditional UAV systems relying on centralized cloud processing suffer from high latency, limited bandwidth, and energy inefficiency. The review investigates how integrating edge computing with AI can mitigate these limitations and enable real-time UAV intelligence.

**Scope:**  
The review focuses on the intersection of:

- Edge computing
- Artificial intelligence
- Unmanned aerial vehicles (UAVs)
- Internet of Things (IoT)

It covers topics such as:

- Edge AI architectures for UAV systems
- Autonomous UAV operations
- Communication frameworks
- Security and privacy
- Energy and power management
- UAV applications across multiple domains

**Main contributions claimed by the authors:**

- Overview of the convergence between edge computing and AI in UAV systems
- Analysis of technical aspects enabling UAV edge intelligence
- Discussion of application domains for UAV edge AI
- Identification of key challenges in deploying edge AI for UAVs
- Presentation of open research directions in this area

The authors highlight several research and development recommendations, including:

- Deploy AI inference closer to UAVs using edge computing infrastructure
- Develop lightweight AI models suitable for resource-constrained UAV platforms
- Improve collaboration between edge nodes and cloud systems
- Address security and privacy concerns in UAV edge computing systems
- Enhance communication efficiency and resource management for UAV networks
- Develop energy-efficient AI and computing architectures for UAV platforms

## 12. Limitations of the Review

The review implicitly acknowledges limitations such as:

- Rapid evolution of UAV and edge AI technologies
- Lack of standardized system architectures
- Limited real-world deployment of UAV edge AI systems

However, the paper does not explicitly discuss methodological limitations of the review itself.

## 13. Methodological Strengths of This SLR

- Provides a comprehensive overview of the convergence between edge computing and AI for UAV systems
- Covers a wide range of UAV applications and technical challenges
- Clearly identifies research opportunities and future directions
- Integrates concepts from edge computing, AI, IoT, and UAV technologies

---

## 14. Potential Methodological Weaknesses

From a systematic literature review perspective, several methodological elements are missing:

- No systematic search strategy
- No search strings reported
- No databases specified
- No inclusion or exclusion criteria
- No PRISMA-based study selection process
- No deduplication procedure
- No structured data extraction protocol
- No study quality assessment

As a result, the work should be considered a **narrative survey rather than a formal systematic literature review**.

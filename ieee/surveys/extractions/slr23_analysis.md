# 2. Scope and Purpose of the Review

**Main Objective**

To provide a comprehensive survey of techniques for enabling **communication-efficient Edge AI**, addressing communication challenges in distributed AI systems deployed at network edges.

**Problem Addressed**

Edge AI systems rely on collaboration among distributed edge devices (e.g., smartphones, IoT devices, vehicles, and edge servers). Training and inference across these devices introduce significant **communication overhead**, which can degrade system efficiency due to:

- Limited bandwidth
- Distributed data
- Heterogeneous computing resources
- Energy constraints
- Privacy considerations

The paper surveys techniques designed to **reduce communication overhead during distributed learning and inference**.

**Scope**

The survey focuses on:

- Edge AI
- Distributed machine learning
- Communication-efficient learning
- Federated learning
- Edge computing systems

The literature is analyzed from two major perspectives:

- **Algorithm-level communication-efficient learning methods**
- **System-level architectures for edge AI**

**Main Contributions**

The authors claim the following contributions:

1. Identification of the **communication challenges in Edge AI systems**.
2. Survey of **communication-efficient learning algorithms**.
3. Survey of **system-level architectures for Edge AI**.
4. Discussion of **future research directions** in communication-efficient edge intelligence.

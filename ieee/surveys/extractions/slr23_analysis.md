# 2. Scope and Purpose of the Review

**Main Objective of the Review:**  
The paper aims to provide a comprehensive overview of recent advances in deep learning accelerators designed for embedded systems. The review focuses particularly on architectures that enable efficient deployment of deep neural networks on resource-constrained devices.

**Problem the Review Claims to Solve:**  
Deep neural networks demand significant computational and memory resources, which makes their deployment on embedded and edge devices challenging. The review discusses architectural and hardware solutions designed to improve performance and energy efficiency for deep learning workloads on embedded platforms.

**Scope:**  
The review covers research related to:

- Embedded AI systems
- Edge AI architectures
- Deep learning hardware accelerators
- RISC-V based accelerators
- FPGA-based implementations
- CNN and transformer accelerators
- Heterogeneous computing architectures for AI

**Main Contributions Claimed by the Authors:**

- A survey of deep learning accelerators designed for embedded environments
- Analysis of RISC-V based processor architectures for AI acceleration
- Review of hardware/software co-design techniques for deep learning
- Discussion of heterogeneous computing platforms for embedded AI
- Identification of future research challenges in embedded AI acceleration

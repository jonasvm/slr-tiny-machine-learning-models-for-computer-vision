# 2. Scope and Purpose of the Review

**Main objective of the review**

Provide a comprehensive overview of Tiny Machine Learning (TinyML) and the emerging paradigm of Tiny Deep Learning (TinyDL), focusing on architectures, deployment techniques, hardware platforms, software frameworks, and application domains.

**Problem the review claims to solve**

Existing literature reviews focus mainly on TinyML or edge AI in general and do not sufficiently distinguish or analyze the transition toward Tiny Deep Learning on resource-constrained devices.

**Scope**

- Tiny Machine Learning (TinyML)  
- Tiny Deep Learning (TinyDL)  
- Edge AI  
- Embedded machine learning systems  
- Resource-constrained devices and microcontrollers

**Main contributions claimed by the authors**

- A unified conceptual distinction between TinyML and Tiny Deep Learning  
- A comprehensive literature synthesis covering more than 200 studies  
- Analysis of architectures, optimization techniques, toolchains, and hardware platforms  
- Benchmark comparisons including metrics such as latency, memory usage, and model size  
- Identification of challenges and future research directions for TinyDL systems

The authors recommend several research directions:

# 12. Limitations of the Review

The paper discusses several limitations in the field, including:

- Lack of standardized TinyML/TinyDL benchmarks
- Limited hardware resources for deep learning deployment
- Insufficient software frameworks for cross-platform deployment
- Security and privacy challenges in edge AI systems

However, the authors **do not explicitly discuss methodological limitations of the review process itself.**

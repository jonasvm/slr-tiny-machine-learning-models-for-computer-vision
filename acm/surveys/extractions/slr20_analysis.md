## 2. Scope and Purpose of the Review

**Main Objective of the Review:**  
To provide a comprehensive overview of early-exit deep neural networks, including their architecture, training strategies, deployment mechanisms, benefits, challenges, and applications.

**Problem the Review Claims to Solve:**  
The literature on early-exit neural networks is fragmented and often described using different terminologies such as cascaded networks, adaptive networks, and multi-exit networks. The paper aims to unify these concepts and organize the research landscape.

**Scope:**  
- Deep neural networks  
- Early-exit neural networks  
- Adaptive inference  
- Edge intelligence  
- Efficient inference acceleration

**Main Contributions Claimed by the Authors:**

- Unifies fragmented literature on early-exit neural networks.
- Provides a structured introduction to early-exit DNN architectures.
- Compares benefits of early-exit DNNs with traditional DNNs.
- Reviews training and deployment strategies.
- Identifies research challenges and application opportunities.

## 11. Practical Recommendations

The paper highlights several practical recommendations for researchers:

- Exit branch placement significantly affects model performance.
- Early-exit policies must balance inference speed and accuracy.
- Side branches should be carefully designed with appropriate neural layers.
- Exit strategies must be optimized for deployment environments such as edge systems.

The authors also emphasize the importance of early-exit models for:

- edge computing
- resource-constrained environments
- distributed inference architectures.

- No systematic search strategy reported.
- Databases used for literature retrieval are not specified.
- Search strings are not reported.
- No inclusion or exclusion criteria defined.
- No PRISMA flow diagram.
- No duplicate removal process.
- No quality assessment of studies.
- No reproducible study selection protocol.

Therefore, the paper should be classified as a **narrative survey rather than a systematic literature review**.

## 2. Scope and Purpose of the Review

**Main Objective:**  
To provide a comprehensive overview of TinyML within the Internet of Things (IoT) ecosystem, discussing the enabling technologies, hardware–software co-design, current research developments, and future challenges associated with deploying machine learning models on resource-constrained devices.

**Problem Addressed:**  
Traditional IoT architectures depend heavily on cloud-based machine learning, which introduces latency, increases bandwidth consumption, and raises privacy concerns. TinyML aims to shift machine learning inference to microcontrollers and embedded edge devices to enable efficient, low-latency, and privacy-preserving intelligent systems.

**Scope:**  
- TinyML  
- Internet of Things (IoT)  
- Edge AI  
- Embedded machine learning  
- Microcontroller-based inference  
- Hardware–software co-design  
- Resource-constrained AI systems

**Main Contributions Claimed by the Authors:**

- Introduction and explanation of the TinyML paradigm  
- Discussion of TinyML technologies and enabling infrastructure  
- Analysis of the role of hardware–software co-design in TinyML systems  
- Review of TinyML tools, frameworks, and platforms  
- Examination of academic and industrial progress in TinyML  
- Discussion of challenges and future research directions for TinyML in IoT environments

## 12. Limitations of the Review

The paper mainly discusses limitations of the TinyML field rather than limitations of the review methodology.

Examples mentioned include:

- Lack of standardized benchmarking frameworks for TinyML  
- Heterogeneous hardware platforms that make comparison difficult  
- Limited memory and energy resources of microcontrollers  
- Inconsistent evaluation setups across studies

No explicit methodological limitations of the review process are reported.

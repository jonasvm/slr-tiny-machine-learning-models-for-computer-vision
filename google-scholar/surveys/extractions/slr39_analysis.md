## 2. Scope and Purpose of the Review

**Main Objective of the Review:**  
To analyze and synthesize existing research on **reformable TinyML**, focusing on techniques that allow TinyML models deployed on resource-constrained edge devices to **adapt or update after deployment**.

**Problem the Review Claims to Solve:**  
Most existing TinyML surveys focus on **static TinyML systems**, frameworks, or domain-specific applications. The authors argue that prior work does not sufficiently explore **model reformability, deployment strategies, and adaptive learning in TinyML systems**.

**Scope:**  
- TinyML  
- Reformable TinyML  
- Edge AI  
- IoT devices  
- Microcontroller-based machine learning  
- Online and incremental learning

**Main Contributions Claimed by the Authors:**

1. A comprehensive survey of **reformable TinyML approaches**.
2. A **novel taxonomy** for classifying reformable TinyML methods.
3. Analysis of **TinyML deployment schemes**.
4. Review of **tools and benchmarking frameworks**.
5. Discussion of **industrial applications of reformable TinyML**.
6. Identification of **research challenges and future directions**.

## 4. Inclusion and Exclusion Criteria

**Inclusion Criteria:**

- The article must be within the **IoT domain**.
- The article must focus on **running ML/DL models on extremely energy-efficient devices such as MCUs**.
- The article must investigate **incremental learning, online learning, over-the-air updates, or collaborative TinyML solutions**.

**Exclusion Criteria:**  
Not explicitly reported.

## 5. Deduplication and Screening Process

The authors describe a general article selection process:

1. Conduct a **keyword-based search across multiple databases**.
2. Review **titles, abstracts, and conclusions** of candidate papers.
3. Verify relevance to **reformable TinyML research**.
4. Perform **reference snowballing**, examining references of selected papers to find additional relevant studies.
5. Apply the defined **inclusion criteria** to determine final selections.

Duplicate removal procedures are **not described**.

Full-text screening procedures are **not explicitly reported**.

## 11. Practical Recommendations

The authors provide several recommendations for future research:

- Develop **adaptive TinyML models capable of online learning**.
- Improve **incremental learning techniques for microcontrollers**.
- Create **benchmarking frameworks specifically for TinyML systems**.
- Design robust **over-the-air model update mechanisms**.
- Explore **collaborative and federated TinyML architectures**.

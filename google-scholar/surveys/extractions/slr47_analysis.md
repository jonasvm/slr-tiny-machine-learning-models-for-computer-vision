## 2. Scope and Purpose of the Review

**Main Objective:**  
To provide a structured survey of model compression techniques used to enable deployment of machine learning models in TinyML environments and resource-constrained edge devices.

**Problem Addressed:**  
Modern deep learning models require significant computational power, memory, and energy, making them difficult to deploy on ultra-constrained devices such as microcontrollers used in TinyML applications.

**Scope:**  
- TinyML  
- Model compression techniques  
- Embedded AI  
- Edge computing  
- IoT devices

**Main Contributions Claimed by the Authors:**

- A structured overview of model compression techniques applicable to TinyML.
- Explanation of key approaches such as:
  - pruning
  - quantization
  - knowledge distillation
  - low-rank factorization
  - neural architecture search
  - weight sharing and Huffman coding
- Discussion of trade-offs and challenges related to deploying compressed models on embedded devices.
- Identification of future research directions for efficient AI at the edge.

## 11. Practical Recommendations

The authors provide several practical insights:

- No single compression method is universally optimal.
- Combining compression methods (e.g., pruning + quantization) often provides better results.
- Compression strategies must consider the target hardware architecture.
- Hardware–software co-design is critical for efficient TinyML deployment.
- Deployment must balance:
  - accuracy
  - memory usage
  - latency
  - energy consumption.

- No systematic search methodology.
- Databases not reported.
- Search queries not reported.
- No inclusion/exclusion criteria.
- No screening process described.
- No PRISMA flow diagram.
- No quality assessment of studies.
- No structured data extraction protocol.
- No quantitative synthesis of results.

Overall, the paper is a **narrative survey rather than a systematic literature review**.

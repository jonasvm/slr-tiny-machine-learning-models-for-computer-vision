## 2. Scope and Purpose of the Review

**Main Objective of the Review**

The paper aims to provide a comprehensive overview of the challenges, techniques, frameworks, and architectures enabling artificial intelligence deployment on resource-constrained IoT and embedded devices, particularly within the TinyML paradigm.

**Problem the Review Claims to Solve**

Deploying artificial intelligence models on edge and embedded devices is difficult due to severe constraints in:

- computational power  
- memory capacity  
- energy consumption  
- latency requirements  
- reliability and security

The review addresses how recent research attempts to overcome these limitations.

**Scope**

The review focuses on:

- TinyML
- Edge AI
- Embedded machine learning
- Resource-constrained AI systems
- IoT-based AI deployment

Application domains discussed include:

- healthcare
- smart cities
- agriculture
- environmental monitoring
- Industry 4.0

**Main Contributions Claimed by the Authors**

The authors claim the following contributions:

- A comprehensive analysis of the challenges of deploying AI on constrained hardware.
- A review of state-of-the-art methods enabling TinyML, including compression techniques and frameworks.
- An examination of applications where TinyML can have significant impact.
- A discussion of security, privacy, and ethical considerations for edge AI.
- Identification of research gaps and future directions for TinyML and resource-constrained AI.

## 11. Practical Recommendations

The authors provide recommendations for researchers working on TinyML:

- Adopt **system-level co-design** between hardware and algorithms.
- Benchmark models under **real resource constraints**, including latency, memory, and energy usage.
- Implement **layered security mechanisms** to protect edge devices.
- Balance **specialized models and adaptable models** for constrained environments.
- Address **ethical concerns, bias, and sustainability issues** in large-scale IoT deployments.

## 12. Limitations of the Review

The paper does not provide a dedicated limitations section.

However, it highlights broader research challenges such as:

- lack of standardized benchmarks for TinyML
- difficulty comparing systems across heterogeneous hardware platforms
- limited evaluation standards for energy consumption and latency

These are presented as field challenges rather than methodological limitations of the review.

## 13. Methodological Strengths of This SLR

Strengths of this review include:

- comprehensive overview of TinyML ecosystem

- system-level perspective connecting algorithms, hardware, and applications

## 14. Potential Methodological Weaknesses

Potential weaknesses include:

- absence of a defined search methodology
- no database selection described
- no search strings reported
- no publication time window defined
- no inclusion/exclusion criteria
- no study screening process
- no PRISMA diagram
- no quality assessment of included studies

Therefore, the paper is better characterized as a **narrative survey** rather than a systematic literature review.

## 2. Scope and Purpose of the Review

**Main Objective:**  
To provide a comprehensive overview of efficient neural network techniques and optimization methods that enable deep learning deployment on extremely resource-constrained devices in Tiny Machine Learning (TinyML).

**Problem Addressed:**  
Deep neural networks are typically computationally intensive and memory-hungry, which makes their deployment on ultra-low-power devices such as microcontrollers difficult. The review explores methods and architectures that make such deployments feasible.

**Scope:**  

- Tiny Machine Learning (TinyML)
- Efficient neural networks
- Model compression
- Deployment on microcontrollers
- Embedded deep learning systems
- Edge AI

**Main Contributions Claimed by the Authors:**

- Overview of efficient neural network architectures for TinyML
- Review of model compression techniques (e.g., pruning, quantization)
- Analysis of knowledge distillation and low-rank factorization methods
- Discussion of TinyML deployment frameworks and tools
- Identification of challenges and future research directions for TinyML

## 11. Practical Recommendations

The authors highlight several practical recommendations for TinyML research and deployment:

- Use quantization (particularly INT8) to reduce model size and computation
- Combine compression methods (e.g., pruning + quantization)
- Consider hardware–software co-design for TinyML systems
- Improve benchmarking practices for embedded AI models
- Develop better end-to-end TinyML deployment pipelines
- Investigate adaptive and resource-aware neural networks

---

## 12. Limitations of the Review

The paper mainly discusses limitations in the TinyML field rather than methodological limitations of the review itself. These include:

- Lack of standardized benchmarks
- Difficulty comparing models across hardware platforms
- Inconsistent reporting of performance metrics such as energy consumption, latency, and memory usage

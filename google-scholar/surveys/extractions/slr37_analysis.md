# 2. Scope and Purpose of the Review

**Main Objective of the Review:**  
The review aims to provide a comprehensive overview of TinyML technologies and their role in enabling machine learning on ultra-low-power embedded devices for large-scale Internet of Things (IoT) deployments.

**Problem the Review Claims to Solve:**  
The increasing number of IoT devices generates massive data streams that cannot always rely on cloud computing due to latency, bandwidth, privacy, and energy constraints. TinyML provides a framework for running machine learning models directly on embedded devices with limited computational resources.

**Scope:**  
- TinyML  
- Edge AI  
- Embedded machine learning  
- Ultra-low-power ML systems  
- IoT deployments  
- Edge computing

**Main Contributions Claimed by the Authors:**

- Overview of TinyML concepts and architecture
- Review of hardware platforms supporting TinyML
- Overview of TinyML software frameworks and tools
- Discussion of TinyML integration with IoT and network technologies such as 5G and LPWAN
- Identification of challenges and future research directions for TinyML systems

# 14. Potential Methodological Weaknesses

Weaknesses in the review methodology include:

- No PRISMA protocol
- No Boolean search query combining multiple research domains
- Lack of clearly defined inclusion and exclusion criteria
- No publication year restriction
- Absence of study quality assessment
- No standardized data extraction template

These characteristics make the study methodologically closer to a **structured narrative survey** rather than a fully rigorous systematic literature review.
